# Feature 019 - Staged ETL and DuckDB Warehouse

Branch: `feature/etl-warehouse`

## Goal

Transform nine-plus years of heterogeneous raw trip files into one clean, documented DuckDB warehouse with a Kimball-style star schema, and publish an honest data-quality report. This is the centerpiece data-engineering artifact of the project.

## Schema Drift to Handle (verified against real files)

| Aspect | 2017 era | Modern era |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Container | XLSX (Excel serial dates) | CSV (`YYYY-MM-DD HH:MM:SS`); two months are Sheets exports |
| Membership column | `Membership type` | `Formula` |
| E-bike flag | absent (no e-bikes yet) | `Electric bike` (True/False) |
| Stopovers | `Stopover duration (sec.)`, `Number of stopovers` | `Lock duration (sec.)`, `Number of bike locks` |
| Extra columns | `Account`, battery voltage columns (drop) | temperature columns (keep; also in 2017) |

`column_eras.json` maps every observed header variant to the unified schema. Extract must fail loudly on a header it does not recognize - new drift becomes an explicit mapping decision, never a silent guess.

## Unified Trip Schema (conform output)

`departure_ts`, `return_ts`, `bike_id`, `is_ebike` (false where the column predates e-bikes), `departure_station_id`, `departure_station_name`, `return_station_id`, `return_station_name`, `membership_raw`, `distance_m`, `duration_s`, `stopover_s`, `stopover_count`, `departure_temp_c`, `return_temp_c`, `source_file`, `quality_flags`.

- Station IDs are the leading 4-digit prefix of station names (`0154 Kitsilano Beach Park` â†’ `0154`); rows without a parseable prefix keep a null ID and are flagged.
- Timestamps are Vancouver local time as published. Excel serial dates are converted during extract.

## Cleaning Rules (flag, do not silently drop)

Every rule increments a named counter in the quality report. Rows are dropped only for: blank departure and return station, unparseable timestamps, or exact duplicates.

- **Dedupe:** monthly files contain spillover trips from neighboring months (verified: a November 2025 departure appears in the May 2026 file), so the same trip can appear in two files. Deduplicate on the full tuple (departure_ts, return_ts, bike_id, stations, duration_s). A trip's canonical month is its **departure** month.
- **Flags (kept in `quality_flags`):** `zero_duration`, `excessive_duration` (> 24 h), `excessive_distance` (> 60 km), `round_trip_false_start` (same station, < 2 min), `negative_duration`, `missing_station_id`, `temp_out_of_range` (< -30 or > 45).
- Published aggregates (spec 020) exclude flagged rows where the flag invalidates the measure, and the methodology says so.

## Star Schema

- `fact_trips` - one row per trip, FKs to dimensions, degenerate quality flags.
- `dim_station` - one row per station ID ever seen in trips; current name, all historical names (array), first/last seen dates, `is_active`; lat/lon and capacity joined from the GBFS snapshot; nearest rapid-transit station and haversine distance computed from the CoV dataset. Retired stations keep null coordinates and are counted in the join-coverage metric.
- `dim_date` - calendar with year, month, day-of-week, weekend flag, season.
- `dim_membership` - raw label â†’ group (365 Member, Monthly, Pay-per-ride, Corporate, Community Pass, Other) via `membership_groups.csv`. Unmapped labels land in the quality report, not in `Other`, so the mapping stays deliberate.

## Data-Quality Report

`docs/data-quality-report.md` is regenerated by `quality_report.py` and committed. It contains: rows in/out per stage per source file; drop and flag counts by rule; duplicate/spillover counts; station-ID join coverage against GBFS (percent of trips whose station resolves to coordinates); unmapped membership labels; min/max timestamps per file versus its expected period; and a short prose summary of known limitations (hour-rounded timestamps, excluded rebalancing trips, retired stations without coordinates).
